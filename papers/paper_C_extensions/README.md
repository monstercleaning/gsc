## Scope

Paper C presents the T4 extension modules. Each module is presented with explicit kill-test, so a reviewer can reject one without affecting the others. Modules carried forward from v9.1, with corrections of dimensional errors documented in [archive/v9_1_deferred_ideas.md](../../archive/v9_1_deferred_ideas.md) (to be ported from `../GSC v9.1/DEFERRED_IDEAS_v10.md`).

### Modules

#### C.1 Vortex Dark Matter from Kibble–Zurek Defect Formation

- The σ_*-crossing as a continuous phase transition in the gravitational sector;
- KZ scaling for defect density;
- Vortex tangle as effective dark matter;
- Predictions: cosmic-string GW background (P6), CMB B-mode signature, dwarf-galaxy phenomenology;
- Kill-test: NANOGrav / EPTA / LISA stochastic GW exclusion at predicted amplitude.

#### C.2 Holographic Proton at the Bekenstein Bound

- AdS/QCD warp factor and the G_s/G_N ~ 10^11 enhancement;
- Bekenstein-bound saturation as origin of σ_*;
- Information-theoretic interpretation of proton stability;
- Kill-test: any measured proton-decay channel structure inconsistent with information conservation.

#### C.3 Information-Thermodynamic Gravity (Verlinde–Bekenstein–Jacobson)

- σ-running as cosmological-information rate;
- Holographic complexity rate as derivation of σ̇/σ;
- σ ≅ exp(-t/τ_RG): time as RG-flow parameter (Connes thermal-time hypothesis);
- Kill-test: structural inconsistency with established holographic-complexity bounds.

#### C.4 Spatial σ(x,t) and MOND Phenomenology

- σ as a field, not just σ(t);
- MOND-like flat rotation curves from σ-gradient force;
- Distinct predictions from generic MOND: cluster vs. galaxy, void dynamics;
- Kill-test: DESI peculiar-velocity surveys finding standard ΛCDM at GSC-predicted deviation level.

#### C.5 σ as Cosmological Quantum Reference Frame

- Reformulation of GSC in Giacomini–Brukner QRF formalism;
- Geometric-lock as theorem about invariance under σ-QRF transformations;
- Decoherence rate of σ-superpositions as observational handle;
- Kill-test: structural inconsistency with QRF formalism (mathematical, not observational).

#### C.6 Vortex DM, Information Thermodynamics, and Their Cross-Consistency

- Whether the C.1 KZ density and the C.3 information-thermodynamic abundance are consistent;
- Failure of cross-consistency would force one or the other module to be dropped, but does not affect C.4 or C.5.

## Discipline

Each module section follows the template:

1. Statement of the claim;
2. Status (derivation, ansatz, conjecture);
3. Independence (which other modules / tiers does this claim require?);
4. Kill-test;
5. If killed, what survives.

This is the operational realization of the tier hierarchy at the module level.
