# Paper B — RG Mechanism for G(σ)

**Working title:** *Renormalization-Group Running of the Gravitational Coupling and Cosmological Scale Field in GSC.*

**Tier scope:** T3 (physical ansatz for G(σ); candidate derivations of σ_*).

**Length target:** ≈ 25 pages.

**Venue target:** Class. Quantum Grav. / JHEP.

**Status:** Outline; depends on FRG calculation collaboration.

## Scope

Paper B presents the physical mechanism for σ(t) evolution: the renormalization-group running of the gravitational coupling. It explores two candidate derivations of σ_* (non-commutative IR/UV mixing; AdS/QCD warp factor), and proposes the σ-θ coupling that connects to the strong CP problem.

### Sections

1. Introduction: from phenomenological σ(t) to physical mechanism;
2. The G(σ) ansatz and its parameter space;
3. Status of σ_* — phenomenological for now;
4. Candidate derivation: σ_* from non-commutative IR/UV mixing;
5. Candidate derivation: σ_* from AdS/QCD warp factor;
6. Coupling of σ to QCD topological sector via RG flow;
7. Cosmological θ-relaxation: σ as effective axion replacement;
8. Predicted CMB cosmic birefringence as consistency check;
9. Bounds from nEDM and atomic-clock constraints;
10. Pre-registered predictions P4 (CMB birefringence) and P5 (strong-CP θ-bound);
11. Open problems and outlook.

## Collaboration notes

This paper is the most theoretically demanding of the four and is a candidate for collaboration with an FRG specialist. The σ-F̃F coupling claim is the highest-leverage open problem in the framework: a positive result would unify dark energy, the strong CP problem, and CMB birefringence under one σ-field; a null result would force the σ-axion-equivalence claim to be dropped from v13.
