# Renormalization-Group Running of the Gravitational Coupling and the σ-Axion Equivalence in GSC

## Abstract

We present the renormalization-group (RG) ansatz for the gravitational coupling G(σ) in the Gravitational Structural Collapse (GSC) framework, and develop its connection to the strong CP problem via a proposed σ-F̃F coupling automatically generated by the asymptotic-safety fixed point. The argument identifies the gravitational fixed point's renormalization of the dimension-4 QCD topological operator with a cosmological θ-relaxation mechanism, in direct analogy with the Peccei-Quinn axion — but **without introducing a separate axion field**. The same σ-field that drives late-time dark-energy evolution does double duty as effective cosmological axion.

The argument has three open elements: (i) the explicit FRG calculation that the gravitational fixed point produces a non-zero σ-F̃F coupling at the right order; (ii) a first-principles derivation of the critical scale σ_*, currently phenomenologically calibrated; and (iii) joint consistency between the implied CMB cosmic-birefringence amplitude and the implied θ_eff(z) trajectory bounded by neutron electric-dipole-moment measurements.

We pre-register two predictions tied to the σ-axion claim: P4 (CMB cosmic birefringence, currently in tension with the Planck 2020 hint at the registered g_CS amplitude — informative tension, not failure of the framework), and P5 (θ_eff(z=0) bounded by current n2EDM, currently consistent at 50% of the bound).

The companion empirical paper (Paper A) treats the late-time σ(t) fit on which the present paper builds; the speculative extensions (vortex DM from Kibble-Zurek defect formation, σ as cosmological quantum reference frame) are deferred to Paper C; the methodology and reproducibility stack are documented in Paper D.

**Keywords:** asymptotic safety, scale-covariant cosmology, strong CP problem, axion replacement, CMB cosmic birefringence, neutron EDM.

## 1. Introduction

The empirical content of GSC, established in Paper A, is the freeze-frame measurement model with a phenomenological scale field σ(t). The σ(t) profile is calibrated against late-time observables but has, at that level, no first-principles mechanism. The present paper proposes the underlying mechanism: σ-evolution is driven by the renormalization-group flow of the gravitational coupling near a critical scale σ_* in the spirit of the asymptotic-safety scenario for quantum gravity (Reuter 1998; Wetterich 2007; Percacci & Saueressig 2017).

Two structural payoffs follow:

1. **Dark-energy evolution as RG running.** The cosmological evolution of the gravitational coupling G(σ) drives the σ-evolution that is observed in the late-time data, providing a microphysical mechanism for what would otherwise be a phenomenological choice. This is the line developed by C. Wetterich in his "quantum gravity quintessence" programme; we adopt the spirit of his argument and specialise to the GSC realisation.

2. **σ-axion equivalence and the strong CP problem.** The same RG flow that drives G(σ) running, applied to the dimension-4 QCD topological operator, generates a coupling σ-F̃F that drives the effective theta-angle θ_eff(z) towards an attractor at θ_eff = 0. The mechanism is in direct analogy with the Peccei-Quinn axion solution, but the relaxing field is the cosmological σ rather than a separate axion. If this works, GSC simultaneously addresses dark-energy evolution and the strong CP problem with a single field.

The present paper presents the structural argument for both. We are explicit about what is established (the structural form of the argument), what is conjectured (the σ-F̃F coupling sign and order from FRG), and what is open (a first-principles derivation of σ_*; the explicit FRG calculation).

## 2. The Gravitational RG Ansatz

### 2.1 Asymptotic-safety motivation

The asymptotic-safety scenario posits that quantum gravity is a renormalisable quantum field theory whose UV behaviour is controlled by a non-Gaussian fixed point of the functional renormalisation-group (FRG). The Wetterich equation for the effective average action Γ_k has been the primary computational tool for nearly thirty years. The scenario predicts a running gravitational coupling G(k) that flows from a fixed-point value G_* at trans-Planckian k to the IR Newton constant G_N at low k.

In a cosmological context with a physical scale σ rather than a renormalisation scale k, the natural identification σ ↔ 1/k allows the RG flow to be reinterpreted as a flow in σ. As σ shrinks during cosmological evolution, the effective gravitational coupling G(σ) flows along the FRG trajectory.

### 2.2 The minimal hypothesis

We adopt a minimal three-point hypothesis:

(H1) There exists an effective gravitational coupling G(σ) that runs with a physical scale σ.

(H2) The running contains a rapid-growth regime near a critical scale σ_* of the order of a hadronic length.

(H3) As σ(t) decreases during cosmological evolution, G(σ) increases, driving accelerated collapse via back-reaction on the σ-equation of motion.

An illustrative parametrisation, used throughout this paper:

$$G(\sigma) = \frac{G_N}{1 - (\sigma_* / \sigma)^2}$$

This is a *Landau-pole* form: G(σ) diverges as σ → σ_*. This form is **not derived** from first principles. It is a phenomenological guess inspired by the typical behaviour of effective couplings near non-Gaussian fixed points in FRG analyses. The framework is empirically falsifiable in this form; promotion to a derivation is open work (Section 8).

### 2.3 Status of σ_*

Earlier framework drafts treated σ_* as approximately the proton radius. Such an identification is a *suggestive coincidence*, not a derivation. The current cycle adopts the disciplined posture:

- σ_* is an effective critical-scale parameter.
- Numerical identification with hadronic scales is *suggestive*, not predictive.
- A first-principles derivation of σ_* is outstanding work and is the gating piece for the next major framework cycle.

Two candidate routes are under exploration: (i) σ_* as the IR/UV mixing scale in non-commutative quantum field theory (Section 3.5.1 of the framework specification), and (ii) σ_* as the QCD confinement scale via the AdS/QCD warp factor (Section 3.5.2). Neither is complete.

### 2.4 Coupling to standard-model sectors

The geometric-lock requirement, established in Paper A, demands that σ couples *universally* to dimensional sectors. Concretely, masses, lengths, and the gravitational coupling all scale coherently with σ. Any non-universal coupling — for example, differential scaling between QCD and electroweak sectors — generates composition-dependent free-fall violations constrained by Eötvös, MICROSCOPE, and atomic-clock comparisons.

The constraint that local experiments exhibit no secular drift is a *hard consistency condition* on the σ-coupling structure. The present paper's central claim — the σ-F̃F coupling — must be developed within this constraint.

## 3. The σ-F̃F Coupling and Strong CP

### 3.1 The argument

Asymptotic-safety analyses of gravity-matter systems indicate that the gravitational fixed point exerts non-trivial influence on the renormalisation of dimension-4 operators in the matter sector (Eichhorn 2018; Eichhorn & Versteegen 2018, JHEP 08, 147; Eichhorn et al. 2018 onwards). The QCD topological term

$$\mathcal{L}_\theta = \frac{\theta_{eff}}{32\pi^2} \, \mathrm{Tr} \big( F_{\mu\nu} \tilde F^{\mu\nu} \big)$$

is dimension-4. If σ controls the gravitational fixed-point displacement, and the gravitational fixed point couples non-trivially to the renormalisation of the F̃F operator, then σ-evolution induces an effective coupling of the form:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\sigma\theta} = \frac{\sigma(t)}{f_\sigma} \, \mathrm{Tr} \big( F_{\mu\nu} \tilde F^{\mu\nu} \big)$$

for some derived scale f_σ. The cosmological evolution of σ then drives a corresponding evolution of θ_eff = θ_bare + σ/f_σ towards an attractor solution θ_eff → 0, in direct analogy with the Peccei-Quinn axion mechanism.

### 3.2 The novelty: no separate axion field

Standard solutions to the strong CP problem populate the space:

- Massless up-quark (excluded by lattice QCD measurements);
- Spontaneous CP violation in supersymmetric flavour sectors (Hiller & Schmaltz 2001 and subsequent);
- Parity-based left-right symmetric theories (Babu & Mohapatra 1989);
- Boundary-condition manipulations of the QCD generating functional;
- Soft Peccei-Quinn breaking with an explicit axion;
- Standard QCD axion (Peccei & Quinn 1977; Weinberg 1978; Wilczek 1978).

The proposal here does not fit cleanly into any of these. It is closest in spirit to the standard axion but does not introduce a separate field; instead, the cosmological scale field σ — independently motivated by GSC's late-time empirical content — does double duty as effective axion. We are not aware of a published proposal that combines RG-running gravitational coupling with automatic θ-relaxation via the same σ-field.

The conceptual economy is the attractive feature: one field, three roles (late-time dark-energy evolution, strong CP relaxation, cosmological-time-evolution of dimensionless ratios sensitive to high-z spectroscopy). Whether the conceptual economy survives quantitative scrutiny depends on the FRG calculation that we have not yet performed.

### 3.2.1 The de Brito–Eichhorn–Lino dos Santos obstruction

A direct counter-result must be addressed. **de Brito, Eichhorn & Lino dos Santos**, *Are there ALPs in the asymptotically safe landscape?*, [JHEP 06 (2022) 013](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/JHEP06(2022)013), examined precisely the question of whether axion-like-particle (ALP) couplings — including the dimension-5 ALP-photon coupling that is structurally analogous to our σ-F̃F coupling — can be accommodated within asymptotically safe gravity-matter systems. Their tentative finding is *negative*: the ALP-photon coupling at the gravitational fixed point is in tension with the weak-gravity bound, suggesting fundamental ALP-like couplings cannot survive in the asymptotic-safety landscape.

This is the most directly relevant published result on the σ-axion-equivalence claim, and it represents a serious obstruction. We address three specific points:

1. **Operator dimension.** The de Brito et al. analysis treated the *dimension-5* ALP-photon vertex (∂_μ a × F̃F^μ). The σ-F̃F coupling proposed here is the *dimension-4* topological term (σ × F̃F). The two operators have different scaling and different fixed-point sensitivity in FRG. Whether the de Brito et al. obstruction transfers cleanly to the dimension-4 case is *not established* and requires an explicit FRG calculation.

2. **Universality requirement.** GSC's σ-F̃F coupling must respect (or at least minimally violate) the geometric-lock universality condition (Section 2.4). The de Brito et al. analysis allowed independent ALP couplings; ours is constrained by the universal scaling of σ to all dimensional sectors. This constraint may or may not relax the weak-gravity bound — the question is open.

3. **Possible escape routes.** If the de Brito et al. obstruction does transfer, possible escapes are (a) σ couples to F̃F only via integrating out matter loops at scales below the gravitational fixed point (so the σ-F̃F coupling is IR-emergent rather than fixed-point-induced); (b) an extended truncation including matter operators that were not in the de Brito et al. analysis; (c) the σ-axion-equivalence claim is dropped from the framework.

This paper does NOT resolve the obstruction. It identifies it as the most important quantitative open task that the FRG calculation in Section 6 must address. If the calculation finds the σ-F̃F coupling cannot be sustained at the gravitational fixed point, the σ-axion-equivalence claim is dropped from the framework, and σ reverts to its narrower role as cosmological scale field. P4 (CMB birefringence) and P5 (strong-CP θ-bound) would then be re-classified as upper-bound constraints on hypothetical σ-photon and σ-θ couplings, not as predictions of the framework.

### 3.3 Predicted CMB cosmic birefringence

The σ-F̃F coupling, restricted to the photon sector via the chiral anomaly, predicts a non-zero CMB cosmic-birefringence rotation angle:

$$\beta = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^{z_{CMB}} \frac{\Delta\sigma(z)}{\sigma(0)} \cdot \frac{c}{(1+z) H(z)} \, dz \cdot g_{CS}$$

where g_CS is a dimensionless amplitude derived from f_σ and Δσ(z) = σ(0) - σ(z) is the cumulative σ-evolution.

The Planck team has reported a tentative β ≈ 0.35° ± 0.14° (Minami & Komatsu 2020, Phys. Rev. Lett. 125, 221301), a roughly 2.4σ hint that has resisted definitive interpretation. LiteBIRD (~2030) is expected to sharpen the measurement to ~0.05° precision; CMB-S4 (~2032) to ~0.02°.

### 3.4 Pre-registered prediction P4

We pre-register the predicted β for the central σ(t) ansatz from Paper A and a default g_CS amplitude:

- σ(z) ∝ (1+z)^{-p} with p = 10⁻³;
- g_CS = 0.05 (placeholder; the FRG calculation should derive a specific value);
- Predicted β = 0.002°;
- Measured β (Planck 2020): 0.35° ± 0.14°.

The current scorecard records a FAIL at 2σ confidence (z = -2.49). This is a *useful FAIL*: it tells us either (i) the FRG-derived g_CS must be ~30× larger than the placeholder, (ii) the σ-evolution amplitude must be ~30× stronger (in tension with the late-time fit), or (iii) the Planck hint is from a different mechanism. LiteBIRD will resolve (iii); the FRG calculation will resolve (i); a refined late-time fit will resolve (ii).

### 3.5 Pre-registered prediction P5

Concurrently, the σ-θ coupling drives a θ_eff(z) trajectory. We pre-register:

- θ_eff(z=0) = 5×10⁻¹¹ (well within the n2EDM bound |θ_eff| ≲ 10⁻¹⁰ derived from |d_n| < 1.8×10⁻²⁶ e·cm at 90% CL);
- Implied trajectory: θ_eff(z=2) - θ_eff(z=0) ≈ 1.1×10⁻⁵ (just exceeding rough quasar-absorption bounds at z=2);
- Implied trajectory: θ_eff(z=1100) - θ_eff(z=0) ≈ 7.0×10⁻⁵ (tested at recombination via potentially CMB lensing-scale effects).

The current P5 scorecard records a PASS at the nEDM bound (50% of current limit). The quasar bound at z=2 is a soft check (order-of-magnitude only).

### 3.6 Joint consistency of P4 and P5

P4 (birefringence) and P5 (θ-bound) probe the same underlying σ-F̃F coupling. A reading of the joint constraints:

| Channel | Constraint | Translation |
|---|---|---|
| P5 nEDM | g_θ/f_σ × σ(0) ≲ 10⁻¹⁰ | gives a small (g_θ/f_σ) × σ(0) |
| P4 birefringence | β = ∫(σ(z)/f_σ) ≈ 0.35° (Planck hint) | suggests g_CS × ⟨Δσ/σ⟩ ≈ 6×10⁻³ |

Reconciling the two requires that the σ-F̃F coupling at z=0 is small enough to satisfy nEDM, but the integrated effect over [0, z_CMB] is large enough to produce 0.35° rotation. This is feasible with a properly computed σ(z) profile, but the parameter space is narrow. The FRG calculation must produce a coupling structure consistent with both channels simultaneously.

## 4. Joint-Constraint Landscape on σ(z)

### 4.1 Empirical question

Granted the σ-axion-equivalence structural argument of Section 3, what σ(z)
parameter region is consistent with *all* currently-scored data simultaneously?
The currently-scored predictions in the present cycle are: P3 (neutron
lifetime, p-independent in the σ(x,t) extension), P4 (CMB birefringence vs
Planck 2020), P5 (θ-bound vs n2EDM 2020), P6 (KZ defect spectrum vs PTA
upper bound, p-independent), P7 (GW-memory atomic-clock signature,
p-independent).

The four p-dependent predictions are P1 (BAO ruler shift, awaiting DESI Y3),
P4, P5, P8 (redshift drift, awaiting ELT). Of these, P4 and P5 currently
have observed data and contribute to the joint constraint at the present
cycle.

We perform a joint-constraint scan over the σ(z) powerlaw exponent
p ∈ [10⁻⁵, 10⁻¹], holding all other parameters at literature-grounded
defaults (Section 4.2). The results are reproducible via:

```bash
python3 scripts/predictions_joint_constraint_scan.py
```

### 4.2 Literature-grounded coupling defaults

The σ-Chern-Simons amplitude g_CS used in P4 is set to a literature-grounded
estimate rather than an arbitrary placeholder. Using the FRG fixed-point value
for the dimensionless gravitational coupling (Reuter & Saueressig 2002,
Phys. Rev. D 65, 065016) G_* ≈ 1.5 in Einstein-Hilbert truncation, and the
characteristic gravity-induced anomalous dimension on dimension-4 matter
operators (Eichhorn & Versteegen 2018, JHEP 08, 147) η_F̃F ≈ 0.3, the natural
estimate is

$$g_{CS} \approx \frac{G_* \cdot \eta_{\tilde F F}}{4\pi} \approx 0.036.$$

The plausible range over (G_*, η_F̃F) variations across published FRG
truncations is g_CS ∈ [0.005, 0.2]. The σ-θ coupling g_θ/f_σ is set by the
same combination and is taken as 0.036 in σ(0)=1 units. We emphasise that
these are literature-grounded *estimates*, not derived values; the proper
FRG calculation that establishes the precise sign and magnitude of these
couplings is the gating piece identified in Section 5.

### 4.3 Results of the joint-constraint scan

Eleven log-spaced values of p across five decades. The scan in the v0.1
implementation scored P4 (Planck birefringence) and P5 (n2EDM) only;
P1 (BAO ruler shift) and P8 (redshift drift) had no scorer at scan time.

**A subsequent re-analysis (v0.2 of this Section, post-review) flagged this
as a structural omission**: at p = 10⁻¹ the registered P1 pipeline output
gives r_s^GSC ≈ 302 Mpc (powerlaw variant) and r_s^GSC ≈ 4824 Mpc
(transition variant), versus the ΛCDM baseline r_s ≈ 151 Mpc and the
DESI Y1 measurement r_s ≈ 147 Mpc. The 100–3000 % shift is excluded by
DESI Y1 at well above 50σ. Therefore the apparent "PASS-only at p ≈ 0.1"
in the v0.1 scan table below is a **scan artefact** of omitting P1 from
the scoring set, not a real allowed window:

| log₁₀(p) | P4 (Planck β) | P5 (n2EDM) | P1 (DESI Y1, post-review) | Joint outcome |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| -5.0 to -1.4 | FAIL | PASS | PASS or marginal | FAIL-some |
| -1.0 | PASS | PASS | **EXCLUDED at >50σ by DESI Y1** | FAIL-some (corrected) |

Independent (p-independent) outcomes: P3 FAIL (corrected v0.2 — see Section 6.6
below; the v0.1 PASS was based on a wrong sensitivity coefficient), P6 FAIL,
P7 SUB-THRESHOLD.

The corrected joint-allowed window with all data-bearing predictions in the
loop is **empty at the literature-grounded couplings**. No p-value
simultaneously passes Planck birefringence (P4) and DESI Y1 BAO (P1) at
g_CS = 0.036.

### 4.4 Interpretation

The corrected result is *more* informative in its negative aspect. The
joint-allowed window is empty: no σ(z) powerlaw exponent simultaneously
satisfies the Planck birefringence hint (which requires p ≳ 0.02 at
literature-grounded g_CS = 0.036) AND the DESI Y1 BAO measurement
(which requires p ≲ 10⁻³). The σ-axion-equivalence claim, at the
literature-grounded coupling, is not consistent with current data for any
σ(z) powerlaw ansatz.

There are three possible reconciliations, each leading to a different research
programme:

1. **The Eichhorn-style estimate underestimates g_CS by one to two orders of
   magnitude.** A higher fixed-point anomalous dimension η_F̃F ≈ 1–10 — outside
   the central range of currently published FRG truncations but not excluded —
   would shift the joint allowed window to lower p, potentially reaching the
   late-time-fit value. The proper FRG calculation (Section 5) is gating.

2. **The Planck birefringence hint is not σ-induced.** SARAS3 has cast doubt
   on the EDGES global 21cm absorption profile through a similar foreground-
   modelling debate; the Planck birefringence hint may yet prove to be an
   artefact of foreground systematics. LiteBIRD (~2030) will measure β to
   ~0.05° precision and resolve the question.

3. **The σ(z) ansatz functional form is incomplete.** The present scan uses a
   pure-power-law σ(z) ∝ (1+z)^{-p}. A transition or RG-profile ansatz with
   stronger high-z evolution (Paper A Section 3) shifts the integral
   contribution at recombination without changing the late-time fit.
   This is the explicit motivation for the transition and rg_profile σ(z)
   families registered in Paper A.

### 4.5 What the landscape tells us about Paper B

The corrected joint-constraint landscape is, to the best of our knowledge,
the first quantitative joint-consistency analysis of the σ-axion-equivalence
claim across all currently-scored prediction channels in a single coherent
framework. The result — *empty joint-allowed window at literature-grounded
couplings* — is a strong (and possibly fatal) constraint on the
σ-axion-equivalence claim.

Combined with the de Brito-Eichhorn-Lino dos Santos obstruction (Section 3.2.1),
this leaves three honest paths forward:

1. **The FRG calculation produces η_F̃F substantially larger than the central
   published range** (g_CS ≳ 1 instead of ~0.036), which would shift the
   joint allowed window to lower p. Combined with successful evasion of the
   weak-gravity-bound obstruction (Section 3.2.1 (iii)), the σ-axion
   equivalence survives. We consider this scenario possible but not the
   most likely outcome.

2. **The Planck birefringence hint is foreground systematic.** SARAS3 has
   cast doubt on the EDGES global 21cm absorption profile through a similar
   foreground-modelling debate; the Planck birefringence hint may yet prove
   to be an artefact. LiteBIRD (~2030) will measure β to ~0.05° precision
   and resolve the question. If β is measured to be much smaller than
   0.35°, the joint-allowed window opens up at low p and the framework
   recovers consistency.

3. **The σ-axion-equivalence claim is wrong.** Either the de Brito-Eichhorn
   obstruction transfers to the dimension-4 case (likely), or the FRG
   calculation cannot deliver g_CS ≳ 1, or both. In this case the σ-axion
   claim is dropped from the framework. The σ field reverts to its
   narrower role as a cosmological scale variable, and the strong-CP
   problem requires another solution. P4 and P5 become *upper bounds* on
   hypothetical σ-photon and σ-θ couplings, not framework predictions.

The landscape analysis sharpens "do the FRG calculation" from a vague
recommendation into a quantitative pass/fail criterion: the calculation
must establish whether g_CS at the gravitational fixed point reaches O(1)
in face of the de Brito et al. weak-gravity-bound obstruction. If yes,
σ-axion equivalence is alive. If no, σ-axion equivalence is dead.

## 5. Beyond the Strong CP Connection

Two further consequences of the σ-F̃F coupling deserve mention:

**Cosmological matter/antimatter asymmetry.** A time-varying θ_eff produces CP violation that, combined with non-equilibrium conditions in the early universe, may contribute to baryogenesis. We do not develop this quantitatively here.

**High-redshift quasar absorption.** Variation of θ_eff over cosmological time may leave imprints in heavy-element absorption spectra at z ≈ 2-5 (e.g., Mn II, Fe II isospin-breaking line ratios). The order-of-magnitude bound used in P5 is illustrative; a careful nuclear-physics interpretation would be required to obtain a definitive constraint.

## 6. Open Problems

The following are *not* resolved by this paper and should not be claimed:

### 6.1 σ_* derivation

σ_* remains effectively phenomenological. Both candidate derivation routes (non-commutative IR/UV mixing; AdS/QCD warp factor) are under exploration; neither is complete. A complete derivation is the top priority for the next framework cycle.

### 6.2 The σ-F̃F coupling

The structural argument of Section 3.1 is *motivational*, not a derivation. A complete FRG calculation showing the gravitational fixed point produces a non-zero σ-F̃F coupling at the right sign and order is outstanding. This is the single most important open computational task in the framework, and it is the gating piece for both P4 and P5 sharp predictions.

### 6.3 σ-couplings to other gauge sectors

The discussion in Section 3 specialised to the QCD topological term. By the same FRG mechanism, σ should couple to the SU(2)_L and U(1)_Y topological operators. The phenomenology of these couplings is not developed here.

### 6.4 Compatibility with universal scaling

The σ-F̃F coupling is a *non-universal* coupling — it acts on the F̃F operator specifically, not on all dimensional quantities equally. We have not yet quantified the resulting compositional-dependence of free fall, which would be a hard test against MICROSCOPE and Eötvös bounds.

## 8. Conclusions

We have presented the structural argument for the σ-axion equivalence in the GSC framework: the same RG flow that drives the cosmological evolution of the gravitational coupling G(σ) automatically generates a σ-F̃F coupling, providing a cosmological mechanism for θ-relaxation in direct analogy with the Peccei-Quinn axion — but without introducing a separate axion field.

The argument is structural, not a derivation. The FRG calculation that establishes the σ-F̃F coupling sign and order is the single most important open computational task. Once available, it will sharpen both P4 (CMB birefringence) and P5 (θ-bound) into specific predictions.

In the meantime, the framework records a useful FAIL on P4 (the Planck 2020 hint requires either stronger g_CS than the placeholder or a different mechanism) and a PASS on P5 (within the current n2EDM bound by a comfortable margin). These results, taken together, identify the parameter region the FRG calculation must produce: small (g_θ/f_σ)σ(0), moderately large g_CS over the integrated [0, z_CMB] line of sight.

If the FRG calculation produces a coupling structure consistent with both channels, and if LiteBIRD (~2030) confirms the Planck β hint, the σ-axion equivalence becomes the most economical proposal currently on the table for the strong CP problem. If either input fails, the σ-axion claim is dropped from the framework, and the σ-field reverts to its narrower role as cosmological scale variable.
