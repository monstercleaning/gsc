## Title
Measurement-Layer Epsilon Inference Diagnostics from SN and BAO: Deterministic Triangle-1 Artifacts

## Authors
Dimitar Baev

## Abstract
We present a deterministic, schema-validated inference artifact for a measurement-layer epsilon parameterization in late-time cosmology. The analysis combines Pantheon+ SN Ia distances, a DESI DR1 BAO summary-likelihood baseline, and a joint SN+BAO Triangle-1 closure diagnostic. Epsilon is treated explicitly at the inference layer; we do not claim a fundamental-physics derivation of time-varying constants from epsilon itself. The repository provides reproducible scripts, pinned manifest checksums, deterministic JSON/PNG outputs, and reviewer-facing verification mapping. We report posterior summaries and chi-square diagnostics with explicit assumptions, including BAO sound-horizon degeneracy handling and compressed-prior scope boundaries.
