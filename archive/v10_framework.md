# GSC Framework v10 - The Renormalized Scale-Dependent Framework

## Overview
- We adopt a static background (Minkowski) with an evolving mass scale \u03c3(t), providing an alternate description equivalent at the level of background cosmology to standard \u039bCDM but with different predictions for time derivatives of the redshift (redshift drift).
- The gravitational coupling G(\u03c3) runs with scale according to a renormalization-group inspired form, G(\u03c3) = G_N / [1 - (\u03c3_* / \u03c3)^2], where \u03c3_* is a critical scale of order the proton radius. This results in strong gravity at small scales without requiring large extra dimensions.
- Universal scaling of all dimensionful constants ensures local measurements (atomic clocks, orbital periods) remain invariant—there is no observable drift in local experiments like GPS or Lunar Laser Ranging. Cosmological measurements relying on look-back, such as redshift drift, do exhibit distinctive signatures.

## Key Sections
1. **Introduction**: Motivation from tensions in \u039bCDM (Hubble tension, cosmic chronometers) and issues with energy bookkeeping in non-static spacetimes. We outline the shift to a static background with running masses and couplings.
2. **Theoretical Framework**: The action includes Einstein-Hilbert term with scale-dependent G(\u03c3) and matter Lagrangians with scale-dependent masses. A dilaton-like field encodes \u03c3(t). The renormalization group form of G(\u03c3) is motivated by Asymptotic Safety.
3. **Scale Covariance and Metrology**: Because masses, lengths, times scale coherently with \u03c3, all dimensionless observables remain invariant. Local experiments comparing clocks or lengths see no drift. The Weak Equivalence Principle is maintained to leading order if gauge couplings and Yukawa couplings scale appropriately.
4. **Cosmology**: The Friedmann equations in the static frame reduce to dynamical equations for \u03c3(t). The effective equation-of-state parameter can cross the phantom divide (w < -1) near \u03c3 \u223c \u03c3_*. Dark energy is interpreted as potential energy release from RG flow as matter collapses in scale.
5. **Observational Predictions**:
    - **Redshift drift**: Unlike \u039bCDM which predicts a negative drift at z\u22673, GSC predicts a positive drift at z>2.5. Next-generation spectrographs (ELT/ANDES) will test this within ~20 years.
    - **Static local universe**: No drift in GPS, atomic clocks, or Oklo, because dimensionless ratios are constant.
    - **Optional modules**: Intrinsic hadronic torsion and superfluid vacuum defects can account for neutron lifetime anomaly and dark matter phenomena, but they are treated as separate modules and not part of the core claim.

6. **Energy Conservation**: We discuss how in a static background, global time-translation symmetry allows a conserved total energy consistent with Noether's theorem, avoiding the "energy non-conservation" issues sometimes attributed to expanding spacetimes.

## Conclusion
GSC v10 presents a parsimonious framework merging renormalization-group running of gravity with a static background and universal scale covariance. It retains compatibility with existing local tests while making a clear falsifiable prediction for redshift drift. This version eliminates earlier speculative elements (ADD extra dimensions, cosmic torsion) and organizes optional phenomena (neutron anomaly, dark matter models) into separate research modules. Future work will refine the RG-derived potential and explore astrophysical implications in more depth.
