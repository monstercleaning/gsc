# Where Does Cosmic Acceleration Come From? — Findings on a Microphysical Origin

**Status:** research findings / honest map of the field, compiled 2026-05-29.
**Framing of the question (per the project's reframed goal):** *not* "get the acceleration for free by changing coordinates," but: **does the observed acceleration of the universe have a real, mechanistic origin rooted in microphysics — instead of being delegated to an unmeasurable "dark energy" fluid?**

The honest headline: **this question is not naive — it is the [cosmological constant problem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmological_constant_problem), widely regarded as the deepest unsolved problem in theoretical physics.** As of 2026, no one has solved it. But understanding *why* it is hard, and which living programs come closest to a genuine micro-origin, is worthwhile — and is the real version of the instinct behind this project.

---

## 0. Why the instinct is right to dislike "dark energy"

"Dark energy" as usually deployed (a cosmological constant Λ, or a tuned scalar field) is not a *mechanism* — it is a number inserted to match the data. The discomfort with that is legitimate and widely shared. The catch is that replacing it with a real mechanism turns out to be extraordinarily hard, for one specific reason (Section 2).

---

## 1. What this project definitively closed: "shrinking matter"

Before looking outward, we close the original idea honestly, because it frees energy for better questions.

- **Universal shrinking = a relabeling of expansion, not a new mechanism.** If all matter shrinks together, only dimensionless ratios are observable, and they are *identical* to standard expanding-universe cosmology (Wetterich 2013, *A Universe Without Expansion*, arXiv:1303.6878). It does not *explain* the acceleration; it moves the same unexplained function from "expansion rate" to "shrink rate." No content is added.
- **Gravity cannot be the "shrinking force."** Atomic size is set by the Bohr radius (electromagnetism + quantum mechanics). Gravity inside an atom is ~10⁴⁰ times too weak to matter. Gravitational clumping is *attractive and decelerating* — the opposite of the acceleration we need, and not the same thing as shrinking.
- **It cannot explain dark matter.** Dark matter's evidence is *spatially structured* — the CMB third acoustic peak (needs ~5× non-light-interacting gravitating mass at z≈1100) and the Bullet Cluster (lensing mass offset from the gas). A homogeneous scale field cannot mimic this; a spatially varying one becomes modified gravity, which fails clusters and the CMB. The one live test of the gravitational law itself — wide binary stars (Chae vs. Banik, 2023–2026) — is currently drifting *toward* plain Newton.

**Verdict: closed as new physics.** What remains worth pursuing is the *question* it was reaching for, below.

## 2. The wall every microphysical-origin attempt hits: the cosmological constant problem

Quantum field theory says the vacuum is not empty — every field has zero-point energy that should gravitate. Summed to the Planck scale, the predicted vacuum energy density overshoots the observed dark-energy density ρ_DE ≈ (2.3 meV)⁴ by **~10¹²⁰**; cut off even at the (well-tested) electroweak scale, still by ~10⁵⁵–10⁶⁰.

- **"Old" problem:** why isn't the vacuum energy catastrophically huge?
- **"New" problem:** given it isn't zero, why is it tiny *and* comparable to the matter density *today* (the coincidence problem)?
- **Weinberg's 1989 no-go intuition:** mechanisms that dynamically relax the vacuum energy toward zero tend to relax *everything* (including the curvature that makes a universe), so you cannot single out "the right small value" without fine-tuning or anthropic selection. Almost every concrete adjustment mechanism since has been caught by some version of this.

**This is why "the acceleration is calculated from microphysics" is not close to achieved in 2026.** The thing microphysics most robustly predicts — vacuum energy — is enormous; the observed acceleration is a whisper. Every program below must climb this wall.

## 4. The idea closest to your instinct that actually works (at the order-of-magnitude level): causal-set "everpresent Λ"

If you want *one* real, alive, quantum-gravity idea where **the acceleration scale emerges from microphysics**, this is it — and it is genuinely beautiful:

- **Mechanism:** Spacetime is made of discrete "atoms" (causal-set theory, a serious quantum-gravity program). In unimodular gravity, Λ is conjugate to the spacetime 4-volume V. Discreteness means V is really a *count* N of atoms, fluctuating by ΔN ~ √N (Poisson). Through the conjugacy this forces a residual **ΔΛ ~ 1/√N ~ 1/√V**, which today evaluates to ~H₀² — exactly the observed scale.
- **Why it's impressive:** It predicted the *magnitude* of Λ from the number of spacetime atoms **before the 1998 supernova discovery**, and it dissolves the coincidence problem (Λ is *always* ~ the ambient density, at every epoch).
- **The honest caveats (why it isn't "the answer"):** It predicts a Λ that *fluctuates in sign* with zero mean — not the steady, positive, near-constant acceleration we actually observe. Turning the slogan into a real cosmology requires hand-built stochastic models (Zwane–Afshordi–Sorkin 2018; Das–Nasiri–Yazdi 2023, arXiv:2307.13743) that, so far, only *tie* ΛCDM for a lucky minority of random seeds and "struggle to improve on ΛCDM" for the CMB. The underlying dynamics that would fix the model don't yet exist.
- **Intriguing 2024–2026 connection:** a temporally *varying* Λ is thematically close to DESI's hint of dynamical dark energy (§6) — though DESI prefers a smooth monotonic evolution, not fluctuations.

**This is the real, learnable, honest version of "the acceleration comes from microphysics."** It is fragile and unproven, but it is genuine physics, not numerology, and far more rewarding to understand than to try to out-theorize.

## 5. The "connect it to micro-physics / step toward a theory of everything" instinct

A classical scale or "shrinking" field does **not** bridge quantum mechanics and gravity. But the deeper aspiration — that *gravity itself emerges from quantum microphysics* — is the hottest real frontier in fundamental physics:

- **Jacobson 1995** — Einstein's equations as thermodynamics (gravity may be emergent/statistical, not fundamental).
- **Van Raamsdonk 2010, "Building spacetime from entanglement"; ER=EPR (Maldacena–Susskind 2013)** — geometry and gravity emerging from quantum entanglement. Solid in toy (AdS) universes; extending it to *our* universe is the open problem.
- **Asymptotic safety** (Reuter, Eichhorn) — the most scale-symmetry-flavored route to quantum gravity, via a UV fixed point.

These are where "a micro-origin of gravity bridging QM and Einstein" genuinely lives — just not via a shrinking field.

## 6. Frontier context (2024–2026): the standard story *is* being questioned

Your instinct that "something is off with dark energy" is, right now, **shared by mainstream cosmology**:

Contested — several 2025 Bayesian analyses find the preference modest and dataset-driven (arXiv:2504.15222; 2511.10631) — but it is the biggest cosmology story in 20 years.

- **Dark *matter*, by contrast, is robust** — the CMB peaks and Bullet Cluster have defeated every "modify gravity instead" attempt at cluster/cosmological scales. The dark-energy door is ajar; the dark-matter door is not.

## 7. Honest bottom line & what is genuinely worth exploring

1. **Your reframed question is the right, deep one** — it is the cosmological constant problem. Asking "where does the acceleration come from?" puts you on the same ground as Weinberg, not on the fringe.
2. **As of 2026, no program derives the acceleration from microphysics.** Sequestering hides the big number but not the small one; holographic DE fits its constant and is DESI-disfavored; emergent-gravity programs relabel the mystery in thermodynamic language; the neutrino-vacuum route was rebutted within weeks (Cline 2024, arXiv:2410.10522).
3. **The closest genuine micro-origin is causal-set "everpresent Λ"** (§4): the acceleration scale from counting the atoms of spacetime. Real, predicted the magnitude, fragile on the details.
4. **Worth *understanding* (as a sharp outsider/communicator), in priority order:** the cosmological constant problem (why it's so hard) → causal-set everpresent Λ → Jacobson thermodynamic gravity → timescape → emergent/entanglement gravity.
5. **Not worth pursuing:** building a "shrinking matter" mechanism (closed, §1); claiming any derivation of the scale (the wall is real and universal).
6. **The honest, brand-positive move** (if communication is a goal): a clear, *true* explainer — *"Why is the universe accelerating, and why is it the hardest problem in physics?"* — riding the live DESI moment, walking through the real candidates and their honest status. That is genuinely interesting, entirely true, and needs no overclaim.

## 7b. Live multi-agent exploration (2026-05-29): a convergence, and its honest dissolution

We ran a full decorrelated agent team — three independent generators (emergent/superfluid vacuum; spacetime discreteness; inhomogeneity) plus an adversarial red-team — at full generative effort, pointed at the fresh 2024–2026 intersections. The *dissolution* is as informative as any candidate, so it is recorded here.

**The convergence (promising at first).** All three generators, from unrelated lenses, independently latched onto the same fact: DESI DR2's hint of an *effective* w(z) crossing −1 near z≈0.5. A single quintessence scalar cannot cross the "phantom divide"; all three conservative mechanisms — Volovik out-of-equilibrium q-theory (and Zloshchastiev's superfluid-vacuum quintom), Sorkin's everpresent-Λ, and timescape/backreaction — produce it naturally. Two cross-references looked possibly novel.

**The red-team dissolved it.**
- **The target is probably not real.** Correcting a ~0.043 mag low-z SNe calibration offset drops the dynamical-DE preference below 2σ (Efstathiou, arXiv:2502.04212); a Monte-Carlo study finds CPL "phantom crossing" exceeds the real-data improvement in only 3.2% of cases (arXiv:2506.15091); and Bayesian model comparison including the DES-Dovekie supernova recalibration finds the dynamical-DE preference reduces to a calibration mismatch, with ΛCDM favored (ln B ≈ −2.8 with Pantheon+; arXiv:2511.10631). The convergence was largely explaining a systematics + parametrization mirage. *(Correction: a round-1 citation "arXiv:2605.13546, Δln𝒵=−2.9" was removed in round 2 as unverifiable — likely a confabulated reference; the conclusion is corroborated by the real paper cited here.)*
- **The convergence was near-tautological.** "A single scalar can't cross w=−1; you need ≥2 effective degrees of freedom" is a textbook no-go theorem (Vikman 2005), not a discovered clue. Phantom crossing is generic for any multi-component / effective-fluid / ratio model — so three ≥2-DOF frameworks all clearing the bar is unsurprising.
- **Postdiction kill-table:** Volovik/Zloshchastiev = quintom-by-construction, ~infinite fitting freedom → **KILL**. Timescape = parameter-light, but its real contribution is the *deflationary* point (the signal is low-z systematics), already mainstream → **PARK**. Everpresent-Λ = the only genuine zero-parameter magnitude prediction (Sorkin, pre-1998), but the DESI link is an unverified interpretive overlay and existing simulations overshoot CMB/BBN → **PARK**.
- **Novelty (corrected in round 2):** the joint ruler+clock-slide idea is already published (arXiv:2409.18668). An initial claim that the everpresent-Λ ↔ DESI-crossing connection was *absent* from the literature was **falsified** by the round-2 curator — causal-set fluctuating-Λ work already makes exactly this link (arXiv:2506.21542). There is **no** novel cross-reference here.

**Honest verdict: no breakthrough.** The one narrow, falsifiable, under-explored question worth a specialist's time — *and only if the signal is confirmed real* — is: *can everpresent-Λ, at its parameter-free amplitude, reproduce DESI's specific w(z) without spoiling CMB/BBN?* Everything else was elegant pattern-matching on a probably-fake signal.

### Round 2 — dialectic to consensus (optimist ↔ pessimist ↔ curator)

Iterating the team past the one-shot red-team — adding an empowered **optimistic critic** and a **curator/screener** — produced a more reliable result, and caught two errors the single pass had let through (a confabulated citation and a false "novelty" claim; both corrected above). - **Volovik / Zloshchastiev → KILLED** (quintom by construction; unbounded fitting freedom).
- **The original 3-way DESI convergence → a correlated artifact**, not a discovery. The curator's diagnosis: three models trained on the same corpus latched onto the same 2024–25 headline (DESI w(z)) plus a *textbook theorem* (Vikman's 2005 phantom-divide no-go) mistaken for a clue. Decorrelation failed at the **input layer** — the genuine fix is to force at least one generator to argue only from *signal-independent* constraints.
- **everpresent-Λ → the one survivor, reframed honestly.** Its real value is **signal-independent**: a pre-1998, parameter-free, order-of-magnitude prediction of Λ from counting spacetime atoms, plus a structural dissolution of the coincidence problem (Λ tracks ambient density at every epoch). The honest open question is **not** the (non-novel, probably-artefactual) DESI link, but whether existing stochastic causal-set simulations **overshoot CMB/BBN** at the parameter-free amplitude — a named calculation for a causal-set specialist (Afshordi/Sorkin school). The optimist also flags an honest caveat: "zero parameters" is really *one structural parameter* (the fundamental density ≈ 1 element/Planck-volume), not tuned to the answer.

**Verdict unchanged: no breakthrough — but a more honest and more reliable consensus, and a clean demonstration that iterating with a screener catches errors a single red-team (and the orchestrator) miss.**

Per the operator's "use the team in a loop" instruction, a second **input-decorrelated** loop (skeptic → generator → verifier, three non-redundant rounds) drilled the one surviving signal-independent question from §7b: can anything bias everpresent-Λ's fluctuating, mean-zero sign to a steady positive value?

- **Jacobson entanglement-equilibrium → KILLED** (category error: it leaves Λ an *undetermined* integration constant — no sign-selection to donate; no bridging literature exists).
- **Anthropics-free volume-weighting selection → KILLED** (known to fail: yields P(Λ=0)=1 / favours short-lived vacua; arXiv:1203.2290, 0706.3201).
- **Unimodular Λ>0 boundary condition (arXiv:2602.19704) → off-target** (selects the sign of a *static* Λ, not the fluctuating one).
- **GSL / horizon-entropy "ratchet" (the round-2 survivor) → KILLED** by the verifier: the thermodynamic gradient runs the **wrong way** — de Sitter is entropically unstable toward *smaller* Λ (*PRD* 33, 1616, 1986); the GSL-monotonicity premise itself fails for a fluctuating Λ (arXiv:1709.08807); and Poincaré recurrence smuggles in the arrow of time the argument claims to derive.

**Verified terminal state: no breakthrough, no novel thread we can own.** The one live calculation is already underway by the specialists who own it; the honest action is to *follow/engage that group*, not to treat this as our frontier. Notably, the loop killed its own most-promising lead (the GSL ratchet) at the verification step — the protocol working as designed.
